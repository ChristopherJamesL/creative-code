CONFIDENTIAL

EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of June 1, 2026, by and between:

TechCorp Inc., a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the State of Delaware,
with its principal place of business at 100 Innovation Drive, San Francisco, CA 94105
("Employer"),

and

Jane A. Smith, an individual residing at 42 Maple Street, San Francisco, CA 94110
("Employee").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Employer desires to employ Employee in the capacity set forth herein, and Employee
desires to accept such employment, on the terms and conditions set forth in this Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein,
and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby
acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

1. POSITION AND DUTIES

1.1 Position. Employer hereby employs Employee as Senior Software Engineer. Employee accepts
such employment and agrees to perform the duties and responsibilities associated with such
position as directed by Employer from time to time.

1.2 Full-Time Service. Employee agrees to devote Employee's full business time, attention, skill,
and effort to the performance of Employee's duties hereunder and shall not engage in any other
employment or business activities without the prior written consent of Employer.

2. TERM

This Agreement shall commence on June 1, 2026, and shall continue until terminated in
accordance with Section 6 of this Agreement ("Term").

3. COMPENSATION

3.1 Base Salary. During the Term, Employer shall pay Employee a base salary of $180,000 per
year ("Base Salary"), payable in equal installments in accordance with Employer's standard
payroll practices, less applicable withholdings and deductions.

3.2 Bonus. Employee shall be eligible to receive an annual performance bonus of up to 20% of
Employee's Base Salary, based on achievement of performance goals established by Employer.

3.3 Benefits. Employee shall be entitled to participate in any employee benefit plans generally
available to Employer's employees, subject to applicable plan terms and eligibility requirements.

4. CONFIDENTIALITY

4.1 Confidential Information. Employee acknowledges that, in the course of employment,
Employee will have access to confidential and proprietary information belonging to Employer,
including but not limited to trade secrets, business plans, customer lists, financial data, product
roadmaps, and technical know-how ("Confidential Information").

4.2 Non-Disclosure. Employee agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and
not to disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without Employer's prior written
consent. This obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5)
years.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

All inventions, discoveries, developments, improvements, and works of authorship created by
Employee during the Term that relate to Employer's business shall be the sole and exclusive
property of Employer. Employee hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in such works to
Employer.

6. TERMINATION

6.1 At-Will Employment. Employment under this Agreement is at-will and may be terminated by
either party at any time, with or without cause, upon thirty (30) days' written notice.

6.2 Termination for Cause. Employer may terminate Employee's employment immediately for
cause, which includes but is not limited to: material breach of this Agreement, gross misconduct,
fraud, or willful neglect of duties.

6.3 Effect of Termination. Upon termination, Employee shall return all Employer property and
Confidential Information in Employee's possession.

7. NON-COMPETITION

During the Term and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination, Employee agrees
not to directly or indirectly engage in any business that competes with Employer's core business
within the United States without Employer's prior written consent.

8. GENERAL PROVISIONS

8.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the
laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

8.2 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with
respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or
agreements.

8.3 Amendments. This Agreement may not be amended or modified except by a written instrument
signed by both parties.

8.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the
remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above.
